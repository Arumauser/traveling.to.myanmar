Famous places in Myanmar

The Shwedagon is a Buddhist reliquary stupa that presents a very distinctive architectural form and purports by tradition to enshrine relics not only of the historical of Buddha, Gautama, but also relics associated with three previous Buddhas, making it distinct from other Buddhist reliquary stupa that contain relics

The Shwezigon Pagoda or Shwezigon Paya is a Buddhist stupa located in Nyaung-U, Myanmar. A prototype of Burmese stupas, it consists of a circular gold leaf-gilded stupa surrounded by smaller temples and shrines.

After the bridge collapsed, the regional government arranged two ferries to carry cars and passengers across the river. Kyaiktiyo Pagoda is a well-known Buddhist pilgrimage site in Mon State, Myanmar. It is a small pagoda built on the top of a granite boulder covered with gold leaves pasted on by its male worshippers. According to legend, the Golden Rock itself is precariously perched on a strand of Lord Buddha's hair.

Inle Lake is in the Shan Hills of Myanmar. At its southwestern edge, Phaung Daw Oo Paya is a tiered pagoda with gilded Buddha statues. In the hills, near the market town of Indein, the stupas of Shwe Inn Dein Pagoda resemble a field of gold spires. The ruined stupas at nearby Nyaung Ohak have been reclaimed by forest. Maing Thauk village is split between land and water, with a wooden bridge connecting the two halves.

The undiscovered coast of Ngapali is best known for its fine white sand beaches, fun water activities, and cultural fishing villages. Bike tours, hot air balloon rides, and cruises are just some of the best ways to see beyond its gleaming shores.
